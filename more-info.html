4. Public Liability Insurance

As part of our Badminton England(BE) affiliation Unicorn has Public Liability Insurance for claims arising against it for Accidental Bodily Injury or Damage. This cover only extends to genuine ‘Visitors’ for up to 6 visits.

Individual Club Members are not covered for their personal liability in any way by virtue of the BE affiliation. It is therefore strongly recommended that Members either join Badminton England themselves or check with their own Household Insurance under the Personal Liability section for possible cover.

It is the responsibility of each Member/Parent/Guardian to decide the extent of cover that is appropriate for them.


5. Personal Information and GDPR

We ask players - adults and juniors - to complete a 'Registration' form which seeks some personal information.

You can download the forms here: Adults / Juniors.

Largely, this consists of your name and contact information so that we can legitimately operate as a club and so that we can contact you directly in the event of needing to cancel sessions at short notice for any reason. We may also contact you about legitimate club business (such as competitive team play or local tournaments), or club-run opportunities (such as a trip and tickets to the All-England Championships). Our affiliation as a club to Badminton England requires us to share certain member information with them. Please see Badminton England's Privacy Notice here (opens in a new window) for further information.

We do ask:

    • for your date of birth (juniors). This assists our age-appropriate working with juniors and with our safeguarding obligations to under-18s.

    • for any relevant health issues. This is optional but will assist the club should there be any health-related incident which requires the club to make interventions in your best interests. It is particularly helpful for coaches working with juniors to be aware of reasonable adjustments they should make to ensure the programmes they use are suitable for every individual player.

    • to allow the use of your photograph. This is optional. We would like to be able to use photographs of players in our promotional material on this website, in our official Club social media posts and in posters promoting the Club.

The Club undertakes to store and process all personal information to the high standards required by GDPR, in particular:

    • to ask for only for the minimum information necessary for the effective running of a club;

    • to store the information you give securely;

    • to correct promptly any errors you tell us about;

    • to enable your "right to be forgotten" when you leave the Club;

    • to use procedures which prevent disclosure (accidental or otherwise) of personal information to others;

    • only to use your information legitimately and for the purposes stated above.

If you have any concerns or complaints about the Club's storage or use of your personal information, please contact the club chair-person.
